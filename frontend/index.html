Sacred Jyotirlingas

First Jyotirlinga

Somnath

Gujarat
The eternal shrine that withstood the tests of time, located on the shores of the Arabian Sea.
Eternal Shrine Sea Coast
Second Jyotirlinga

Mallikarjuna

Andhra Pradesh
Located on Srisailam hills, this is a unique site serving as both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peeth.
Hill Temple Shakti Peeth
Third Jyotirlinga

Mahakaleshwar

Madhya Pradesh
The only Jyotirlinga facing south (Dakshinmukhi), famous for its divine Bhasma Aarti.
Dakshinmukhi Bhasma Aarti
Fourth Jyotirlinga

Omkareshwar

Madhya Pradesh
Located on the Om-shaped Mandhata Island in the Narmada River.
Om Island Narmada River
Fifth Jyotirlinga

Kedarnath

Uttarakhand
Situated at 3,583 meters in the Himalayas, accessible only 6 months a year.
Himalayan High Altitude
Sixth Jyotirlinga

Bhimashankar

Maharashtra
Nestled in the dense forests of the Western Ghats, surrounded by wildlife sanctuary.
Wildlife Forest Temple
Seventh Jyotirlinga

Kashi Vishwanath

Uttar Pradesh
The most famous Jyotirlinga in Varanasi, with gold-plated spires and the divine corridor.
Golden Spires Varanasi
Eighth Jyotirlinga

Vaidyanath

Jharkhand
Known as Baba Baidyanath Dham, famous for miraculous healing powers and Kanwar Yatra.
Healing Powers Kanwar Yatra
Ninth Jyotirlinga

Nageshwar

Gujarat
Located on the Saurashtra coast, featuring a 25-meter Shiva statue and protection from poisons.
Coastal Giant Statue
Tenth Jyotirlinga

Trimbakeshwar

Maharashtra
Unique three-faced lingam representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva at the origin of Godavari.
Three-faced River Origin
Eleventh Jyotirlinga

Rameshwaram

Tamil Nadu
Jyotirlinga established by Lord Rama, featuring India's longest temple corridor.
Rama's Temple Corridor
Twelfth Jyotirlinga

Grishneshwar

Maharashtra
The final Jyotirlinga near UNESCO World Heritage Ellora Caves, embodying compassion.
UNESCO Compassion

About Jyotirlingas

The 12 Jyotirlingas are the most sacred shrines of Lord Shiva, representing his infinite nature as columns of light (Jyoti). Visiting these shrines is considered the ultimate pilgrimage for devotees.
