Jyotirlinga & Shakti Peeth

Vaidyanath Temple is unique in the Hindu world. It is the only place where the **Jyotirlinga** (Shiva) and **Shakti Peeth** (Parvati's Heart) coexist.

Millions flock here, especially during the holy month of Shravan for the **Kanwar Yatra**, carrying holy Ganga water over 100km to offer to the Lord.

Timeline of Legend

Ravana's Penance

Ravana worshipped Shiva to take him to Lanka. Shiva gave him the Atmalinga on the condition it must not be placed on the ground.

The Installation

Deceived by the gods, Ravana placed the Lingam here. Unable to lift it again, it remained fixed as Vaidyanath.

16th Century CE

Raja Puran Mal of Gidhaur built the current main temple structure, showcasing intricate stone carvings.

Modern Era

The temple complex expanded. It is now a major pilgrimage center managed by the Shrine Board.

Architectural Marvel

Lotus Shikhara

The main spire is 72 feet high and features a unique lotus-shaped amalaka and three ascending gold vessels.

Swayambhu Lingam

The top of the Lingam is slightly broken, attributed to Ravana's attempt to uproot it.

Shakti Peeth Connection

The shrines of Shiva and Parvati are connected by red silken threads, symbolizing their eternal union.
